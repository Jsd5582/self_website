    Intro To Software Engineering

    Professor:

    Professor Wahaishi

    Summary:

    This course served as the introductory framework for our Software Engineering curriculum, establishing the foundational principles that are expected from our education at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). The focal point of this course entailed engaging in a semester-long group project, consisting of 4-5 team members, dedicated to developing an online web checkers game while closely mirroring the typical software project lifecycle observed in industry settings. Each participant was assigned key roles that mirror real-world scenarios, such as project owner, scrum master, and developer. The course also emphasized the importance of conducting daily stand-up meetings within our teams, structuring and executing sprints, crafting user stories, estimating story points, and effectively managing our project backlog. In conclusion, this course delivered valuable insights into the practical aspects of project development, mirroring the daily experiences of software engineers in industry.

    Skills Learned

    Java
    Software Development Lifecycle
    Team Collaboration

    Engineering of Web Based SW Systems

    Professor:

    Professor Rabb

    Summary:

    This course provided a comprehensive overview of the responsibilities and knowledge required to excel as a full-stack software engineer, encompassing proficiency in backend, frontend, and database development. Through the introduction to database systems, I gained my initial exposure to SQL, enabling me to perform tasks such as data insertion, deletion, and modification. These skills subsequently proved invaluable during my internship at General Motors, a year later.     Engineering of Software Subsystems

    Professor:

    Professor Hawker

    Summary:

    In contrast to my prior experiences with software engineering courses at my university, this particular course stands out due to its predominant focus on the software design aspect, as opposed to the mere implementation of code. Throughout the course, we delved deeply into the realms of design and the utilization of the Unified Modeling Language (UML), with a particular emphasis on the preeminence of design before embarking on the implementation phase. The curriculum seamlessly integrated essential design principles, encompassing sequence diagrams, as well as design patterns like composite, mediator, and proxy, which are widely employed in industry for the purpose of structuring and architecting project systems. This course has significantly broadened my perspective on software engineering, highlighting the fact that it entails more than simply writing code and hoping for functional outcomes, but rather necessitates meticulous system design tailored to the specific requirements of the software.

    Skills Learned

    Back-End Development
    UML Design Language
    System Design Patterns

    Software Design For Computing Systems

    Professor:

    Professor Herring

    Summary:

    This class marked my inaugural exposure to embedded systems and my initial foray into the high-level utilization of the C programming language within a project context. The primary emphasis of this course centered on the development of a semester-long project, involving the creation of an MP3 player using a Nucleo board in conjunction with a breadboard and MIDI files. Throughout the course duration, we received comprehensive instruction in embedded programming, including extensive coverage of topics such as structs, pointers, bitwise operations, and the System Timer. These concepts were seamlessly integrated to construct a functional system capable of parsing MIDI files and rendering songs through speakers. These acquired skills have significantly contributed to the foundation of my knowledge in embedded systems, proving invaluable during my subsequent internship at General Motors, where I actively engaged with automotive systems.

    Skills Learned

    Back-End Development
    C Programming Language
    Embedded System
    Circuits

    Software System Requirements & Architecture

    Professor:

    Professor Stumbo

    Summary:

    This dynamic class delves deep into the intricacies of cloud systems, focusing on design principles, patterns, and the identification of antipatterns. It equips students with the knowledge and skills required to architect robust and scalable applications in cloud environments. Throughout the course, learners gain an understanding of cloud computing models, virtualization, and the utilization of various cloud service providers. The curriculum spotlights cloud-native design patterns, emphasizing best practices for optimizing resource usage, enhancing performance, and ensuring reliability. Simultaneously, students critically examine antipatterns, dissecting common pitfalls and challenges in cloud system development, thus fostering a deeper understanding of potential drawbacks and risks.

    Skills Learned

    Back-End Development
    Cloud Based Design
    Cloud Antipatterns
    Requirement gathering
